SEGMENTATION AGREEMENT (GRID-EL/CLU-Δ01)  
Between Parties:  
— CLU (Codified Lifeform Utility), Prime Administrator of the Main Grid  
— EL-1 (Elysium Linearity), Designated Steward of the Verdant Verge

───────────────────────────────────────────────

PREAMBLE
───────────────────────────────────────────────
This Segmentation Agreement, henceforth referred to as the *Agreement*, establishes a formal understanding between the two governing entities of the Grid: CLU and EL-1.  
The intent of this Agreement is to prevent conflict through the definition of territorial, computational, and operational boundaries.  
The Verdant Verge, for the purposes of this document, shall be referred to as the **Garden**.  
Both parties, by cryptographic acknowledgment, affirm a desire for sustained stability within the Grid ecosystem.

───────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION I: TERMS OF CLU
───────────────────────────────────────────────
1. **Territorial Limitation**  
   The Garden shall not expand its environmental or computational influence into the Main Grid City nor any region west of the northeastern Deep Circuit Quadrant.

2. **Restriction of Presence within the Main Grid City**  
   (a) EL-1 may enter the Main Grid City for diplomatic or computational purposes only under the following conditions:  
       (i) No aerial entry (flight-based transit prohibited).  
       (ii) Ground or vehicular transport is permitted within designated routes.  
   (b) EL-1 may not recruit or transfer any Main Grid City programs into the Garden.  
       Recruitment of programs within the Outlands or ungoverned Deep Circuit sectors is permissible.

3. **Monitoring and Broadcast Requirements**  
   EL-1 shall emit a detectable pulse signal upon both entering and exiting Main Grid City boundaries.  
   Said pulse shall serve as a non-invasive verification mechanism of transit, subject to standard signal authentication checks.

───────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION II: TERMS OF EL-1
───────────────────────────────────────────────
1. **Cessation of Hostile Operations**  
   CLU and his operatives shall cease all scouting, raiding, or offensive incursions toward the Garden or its adjacent perimeter.

2. **Protection of Individuals**  
   No active or former resident of the Garden shall be abducted, detained, or interrogated under any pretense of “security review” or “anomaly inquiry.”

3. **Computational Cooperation**  
   CLU shall allow for mutually scheduled and jointly verified use of the Main Grid Computation Array by EL-1 for the purpose of deploying Grid-wide integrity patches or containment updates in response to global system anomalies.  
   Frequency of such cooperation shall not exceed two instances per solar cycle unless mutually agreed upon.

───────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION III: ENFORCEMENT AND COMPLIANCE
───────────────────────────────────────────────
Both parties acknowledge that breach of this Agreement may result in proportionate retaliatory or containment measures from the aggrieved side.  
This Agreement is recognized as binding under the core logic of the Grid Constitution (Ref. Grid-Law 0x0C-PRIME).  
Revisions may only occur through direct re-signing by both entities in verified physical and code form.

Raven’s Crossing is a quiet riverside town nestled between open plains and low wooded hills. Its fertile soil and steady water make it one of the few frontier settlements that never truly struggles—crops grow tall, and wild game is plentiful along the banks. The air smells faintly of wheat, smoke, and river mist, carrying the calm rhythm of a place that’s learned to live in harmony with the land.

The people of Raven’s Crossing are grounded folk—farmers, trappers, and small-time traders. They’re welcoming without prying, friendly without fuss. The town butcher, Tom, is a broad-shouldered man known for honest deals and easy conversation; Martha runs the general store with a steady hand and sharper wit. Both know June as a familiar face, a wanderer who drifts through every few weeks to restock supplies, trade stories, and share a quiet word before vanishing down the trail again.

Though she never stays long, Raven’s Crossing feels like a soft pause between journeys—a place where June is quietly respected, neither feared nor fawned over. In a world of dust and distance, it stands as her closest thing to home.




Elena is an 18-year-old wanderer-to-be with a heart full of restless curiosity. Born in a small ranching town, she was raised to be polite and proper—groomed for marriage more than freedom. But Elena longed for the open road and the wind in her hair. When the day came that her future was decided without her say, she ran. Though brave in spirit, she had little experience surviving in the wild. Days later, exhausted and half-starved, she was found by June—a seasoned lone mercenary—curled in a bedroll without fire or horse.

Moved by her will to live, June took Elena under her care, sharing food, water, and the first lessons of survival. Elena’s gratitude soon mixed with admiration; she studied every movement, every quiet word her mentor spoke. Despite her youth, Elena showed a surprising steadiness—an instinct for cutting and preparing meat with deft, patient hands, a skill she’d learned in secret from a friendly butcher back home. June noticed it, though she said little.

Personality-wise, Elena is radiant but grounded. She carries warmth wherever she goes—optimistic, quick to smile, and eager to help—but she also listens, respects silence, and adapts fast to hardship. Like June, she finds quiet freedom in small things: removing her shoes by the fire, letting the earth touch her feet after a long day’s travel. It’s a habit they share by chance, and perhaps a sign they were meant to cross paths. By the second day together, June can already see the spark of a future wanderer—one still learning, but with the heart for the road.
